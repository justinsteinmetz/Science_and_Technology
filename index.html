Technology

Every technology arrives carrying a promise. Every technology changes something else as well. This strand asks what technological change actually does — to knowledge, to labour, to authorship, to the gap between what is known and what is believed.

What the Instruments Share
Design principle 1
Cases before framework
Students encounter specific, documented, real cases before any analytical framework is named. The framework that emerges from encounter is more durable than the framework that arrives as instruction.
Design principle 2
Gain and loss simultaneously
The gain and loss in every case come from the same change — not as opposites, but as the same event seen from different positions. The instrument holds both without resolving them.
Design principle 3
Gained by whom / lost by whom
The question is not whether progress was beneficial in aggregate. It is who specifically gained and who specifically paid — and whether those are the same people.
Design principle 4
Success is not consensus
Students who leave with more precise, more honest, more complicated versions of the positions they arrived with have had a successful encounter. Students who leave unchanged have not.
